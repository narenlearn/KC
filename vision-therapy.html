What is Vision Therapy?

Vision therapy is a series of exercises and activities tailored to the individual needs of each patient. It involves using specialized tools such as lenses, prisms, filters, computer programs, and therapeutic equipment to enhance the visual system's ability to correct itself. Unlike glasses or contact lenses, vision therapy aims to treat the underlying visual skills necessary for reading, learning, and daily activities.

Who Can Benefit from Vision Therapy?

  • Eye coordination problems (Strabismus)
  • Lazy eye (Amblyopia)
  • Convergence insufficiency (difficulty focusing both eyes together)
  • Eye strain and headaches
  • Double vision
  • Visual perceptual disorders
